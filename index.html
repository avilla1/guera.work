We all shout for stout! Rich in history and flavor, celebrate with one (or two) of these dark brews on International Stout Day. First mentioned as “stout porters” in 18th-century England, this hearty draft made its debut as a hoppy, malty aged beer in response to the young, pale ales that were common at the time. As darker, boozier variations made their way across the Atlantic, brewers established a range of styles, from oatmeal to milk to imperial stouts. All are a perfect way to welcome the coziest season! Our favorite lately? The Malice Imperial Stout. This silky, full-bodied concoction from @BombasticBrewing contains notes of vanilla, cacao, and most importantly, coffee. Suggested pairings: shepherd’s pie, french vanilla ice cream, and chilly fall evenings by the fire. Which stout makes you shout? Let’s talk about the faux elephant in the room: the Impossible Burger. In theory, a plant-based patty that looks and tastes like real beef sounds enticing... but there’s a catch: this franken-food isn’t good for humans or the planet. High in saturated fats and sodium (over 4.5x that of ground beef), these overprocessed patties rely heavily on patented ingredients and heavy soy farming on natural grasslands for mass production. Impossible Burgers feature heme, which is “a genetically engineered ingredient that helps the burger look and taste like meat.” The synthetic biology at play in Impossible Burgers may be high-tech, but the results are not “natural” or “sustainable”... not to mention, they’re potentially harmful to our health and the livelihood of farmers. Their CEO has been vocal against regenerative organic agriculture, calling regenerative grazing the "clean coal of beef" – a not-so-popular statement among the carbon ranchers taking the holistic, grass-fed approach to beef. Try this meat alternative instead, available at Pilgrim’s! Sunshine Burger garden herb veggie patties are gluten and soy free and made from unpatentable ingredients. Both USDA Organic certified and Non-GMO verified, they’re full of the goods, not the bads. What are your go-to meat alternatives? Sound off in the comments! Interested in learning more about the new wave of GMOs, synthetic biology? Check out http://www.synbiowatch.org/.
